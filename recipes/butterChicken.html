Butter Chicken

Description

A popular and simple indian meal that is enjoyed by many all over the world!

Butter chicken is believed to have been invented during 1930 to 1935 and was a simple way to use up any left over dry Tandoori Chicken. This chicken would be diced and simmered in a creamy and buttery tomato gravy and commonly eaten alongside Naan or rice.

Follow along and learn how to make this delicious meal in just under an hour!

Ingredients

Chicken Marinade

  • 1 1/2 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts (cut into ¾ inch cubes)
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger (grated, minced, or paste)
  • 1 1/2 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Sauce

  • 2 tbsp ghee or unsalted butter
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 1 1/2 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp ginger (grated, minced, or paste)
  • 14 oz crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste

    • Steps

    1. In a large mixing bowl, add yogurt, ginger, minced garlic, cumin, garam masala, turmeric, and chili powder. Season with a pinch of salt. Stir to combine everything.
    2. Add the chicken into the yogurt mixture and mix to coat the cubes evenly. Seal the bowl with foil or plastic wrap. Set aside and marinate in the fridge for at least 30 minutes to 2 hours. Better if overnight
    3. In a large pan or pot, heat oil over medium-high heat. Divide the chicken into 2 to 3 batches. Once the oil is hot add a batch of chicken and saute for 4 to 6 minutes. Stir frequently to prevent burning.
    4. Set the first batch aside and continue to repeat the process until all batches of chicken are sauteed.
    5. In the same pan add 2 tablespoons of butter. Add the ginger and onions and saute for 4 to 6 minutes. Make sure to scrape up any brown bits in the pan.
    6. Add the garlic, garam masala, and cumin. Saute for another minute or until fragrant. Stir constantly.
    7. Pour the crushed tomatoes into the pan and season with the chili powder and some salt. Simmer for about 15 minutes or until the sauce thickens up and becomes a deep brown-red color.
    8. Adjust the sauce’s consistency with a few tablespoons of water if needed. Add the chicken, sugar, and heavy cream to the pan. Stir constantly and cook for another 8 to 10 minutes until the sauce has reached the desired thickness.
    9. Sprinkle the coriander leaves and turn the heat off.
